## üî¨ Deep Dive: Why AI Agents Keep Failing

Everyone wants AI agents. Few deliver.

The problem isn't intelligence. Current models are smart enough to handle most tasks. The problem is **reliability**.

An agent that works 90% of the time is useless. Would you trust a car that crashes 10% of trips? A calculator that's wrong every tenth equation?

Humans tolerate imperfection from other humans because we understand human failure modes. We don't have intuition for AI failure modes. When an AI screws up, it feels random. Inexplicable. Scary.

The companies winning at agents aren't the ones building smarter AI. They're the ones building more predictable AI. Constrained action spaces. Clear feedback loops. Humans in the loop at critical points.

Boring wins. Flashy demos don't ship.
